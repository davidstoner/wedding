The Wedding

We are pleased to invite you to attend our wedding at the Figge Art Museum on August 4th, 2018 at 6:00 PM. We've been dating for nearly a decade and decided it was time to get all of our friends and family together to celebrate. We hope you can join us for the evening.

Common Questions

  • Where is the ceremony and reception?

    We will be having both the ceremony and reception in the Grand Lobby at the Figge Art Museum (Google Maps) in downtown Davenport, Iowa.

  • What hotel can I stay at?

    We have room blocks with a discounted rate offered at the Radisson Quad City Plaza (Google Maps), a downtown Davenport hotel located within two blocks of the Figge Art Museum. Please call the hotel's reservations department at 1 800 333 3333 or the hotel directly at 1 563 322 2200 and reference the Ebener/Stoner wedding block to make reservations.

    The hotel cannot guarantee any availability but is offering the discounted rate as long as they have rooms until July 4th, 2018. Please keep this in mind and book your accommodations early to ensure there are still rooms available.

  • Where is the best place to park?

    There is a large free parking lot directly across from the museum. It's located south of the venue (toward the river) on 2nd Street. Guests staying at the Radisson Hotel will be within walking distance of the venue.

    There is also a lot directly in front of the museum, but we ask guests to leave this space open for those with limited mobility.

  • What food will be served?

    The catering for the reception will feature a handful of different options. There will be three stations: a slider station with beef brisket, pulled pork, and crab cakes; a macaroni and cheese station; and a Mexican food station. You can choose to mix and match from any of these options. There will also be cupcakes.

  • What's the drink situation?

    There will be a limited number of drinks prepaid, but a cash bar will be available for the rest of the night.

  • What's the dress code?

    You can wear whatever you're comfortable in, but please keep it on the formal side if possible.
